Guidelines

When to Use

  • To reduce a user's uncertainty. Reassure the use that your app is working.
  • To reduce the user's perception of time and offer them a reason to wait.

When NOT to Use

  • Inside of smaller elements, such as buttons, chips, or badges.

Visual language

To inform judgment about color, type, space, and more
  • A progress indicator is represented by a gray bar that is filled with color over time as loading occurs.

Behaviors

To cover how to cope with events, outcomes, and transitions
  • A progress indicator should fill up to completion as background loading tasks are complete.
  • Whenever possible, show the user a textual representation of their progress that corresponds to the loading indicator.

Editorial

Voice and tone, advice covering copy and images flowing into each element
  • Progress indicators generally have negative connotations among users. Use them sparingly. Focus on optimizing loading rather than growing dependant on using progress indicators.
